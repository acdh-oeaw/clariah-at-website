The ‘Digital Library’ conference series is celebrating its anniversary this year in Graz, Austria!

One year after the launch of the European portal ‘[Europeana](https://www.europeana.eu)’, the first conference in 2010 was dedicated to the Europeana initiative for the digitisation of European cultural heritage.

Since then, this series of events - approximately held every two years - has provided a platform for the presentation of national and regional approaches and projects for making digital cultural heritage accessible. The event became a landmark for the creation of a **common platform for the exchange of experiences from all cultural and scientific fields** (museums, libraries, archives, educational institutions, etc.).

On the occasion of the 10th anniversary, the 'Digital Library' wants to look back and into the future.

* Which topics continue to accompany us, which issues have been resolved, what new challenges do we face now and in the near future?
* Can the developments of recent years serve as a guide for future concepts and approaches?

The following topics will also be discussed in lectures, posters and workshops:

* Technology: established and current (e.g.: AI, machine learning, ...)
* Networks: Cooperations, partnerships, collaboration today\&tomorrow\...
* Sustainability: How do we secure knowledge? Verifiability of knowledge? New ways of knowledge transfer?

The programme for the conference can be found [here](https://conference.ait.co.at/digbib/public/conferences/10/schedConfs/12/program-de_DE.pdf) (.pdf).
